What is the Jupiter atmosphere made of?

Jupiter's atmosphere is composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and small amounts of methane, ammonia, and water vapor.


Potential for Life?

Jupiter has little potential for life due to its lack of a solid surface and extreme radiation environment.


What is the gravity on Jupiter compared to Earth?

Jupiter's gravity is about 2.5 times stronger than Earth's gravity. You would weigh more than double your Earth weight on Jupiter.

surface of Jupiter :
Jupiter's surface is made of thick clouds of gas and liquid, with no solid surface. The planet is known for its storm systems, including the Great Red Spot.
Great Red Spot:
A massive storm on Jupiter, the Great Red Spot has been raging for at least 400 years.
Jupiter's Moons:
Jupiter has 79 known moons, including the four largest, the Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.





Why is it difficult to explore Jupiter?


Exploring Jupiter is challenging due to its extreme size, intense radiation, and lack of a solid surface to land on. Missions like NASA's Juno spacecraft are working to understand Jupiter's atmosphere and magnetic field.
